People Killed by Police

shiny Data Exploration App

Josh Morel
Data Scientist

Background

In response to the string of highly publicized police killings of African Americans in the United States, The Guardian initiated a project to build a more comprehensive record of people killed by police in the United States. This project, The Counted, provides a very interesting data set to expand upon for further investigation on a very timely topic - police brutality in the United States.

The Data

The primary data of interest is Rate of People Killed by Police by Population. As this plot shows, Wyoming, New Mexico & Oklahoma are distinct outliers. Further analysis could identify what confounding variables might be common to these states explaining the higher rate. This plot is rendered in ggplot2. I ran into too much difficulty getting ggvis to work in slidify, unfortunately.
